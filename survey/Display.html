The Stelvio National Park is a magnificent protected area located in northern Italy. It spans across three Italian regions: Lombardy, Trentino-Alto Adige, and Veneto. Established in 1935, the park takes its name from the majestic Stelvio Pass, which lies within its boundaries. The pass is famous for its scenic beauty and is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

The Stelvio National Park is a protected area located in the Italian Alps, covering an area of over 1,300 square kilometers. It is the largest national park in Italy and one of the largest in Europe.

It is characterized by a stunning natural beauty, with high mountains, alpine meadows, forests, lakes, and rivers. It is home to a diverse array of plant and animal species.

The park offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, cycling, and mountaineering, as well as opportunities for wildlife watching and cultural exploration.
